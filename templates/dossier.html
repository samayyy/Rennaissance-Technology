04. WHERE DO WE CREATE VALUE?

Active & Pasive

Nowadays, there are two major ways of asset management: Active & Pasive

Active management is the use of a human element, such as a single manager, co-managers or a team of managers,to actively manage a fund’s portfolio.

Active managers rely on analytical research, forecasts, and their own judgment and experience in making investment decisions on what securities to buy, hold and sell.

Passive management believe in the efficient market hypothesis. It states that at all times markets incorporate and reflect all the information, rendering individual stock picking futile. As a result, the best investing strategy is to invest in index funds, which have historically outperformed the majority of actively managed funds.

We beleive that both strategies are complementary. We focus on Active management through our AI system to deliver Alpha to portfolios managers. It is an alternative investment style within the active management.

The concept of alternative investing is really about going beyond what a traditional 60/40 portfolio might look like. Alternatives provide access to sophisticated investment strategies and types of investments that cross asset classes, broaden diversification opportunities and potentially widen portfolio correlations.

As alternative investments behave diferent from traditional portfolios, broaden diversification, lower risk and increase profits.

From 1990, a traditional portfolio (60/40) is correlated with the market up to 90%.
